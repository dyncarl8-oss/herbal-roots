Welcome to the island apothecary. For centuries, the people of the Caribbean have relied on
the rich, biodiverse flora of their islands not just for flavor, but for healing. This tradition of
"bush tea"—infusions made from roots, bark, flowers, and leaves—is a cornerstone of
Caribbean wellness.
This guide will take you beyond the simple cup of tea and into the world of powerful,
immune-boosting tisanes. You will learn about the unique properties of ingredients like the
West Indian bay leaf, turmeric, and sorrel, and how to combine them into potent blends that
have been used for generations to ward off illness and maintain vitality in a tropical climate.
Module 1: The Caribbean Ingredient Encyclopedia
Before you brew, you must know your ingredients. These are the stars of the Caribbean
natural pharmacy, each with a unique profile and health benefit.
● The West Indian Bay Leaf (Bois D'Inde): Distinct from the common European bay leaf,
this variety is known for its intense, aromatic, and warming properties. It is traditionally
used to support respiratory health and clear congestion.
● The Roots (Ginger & Turmeric): These are the anti-inflammatory powerhouses. Ginger
is famous for soothing digestion and warming the body, while turmeric contains
curcumin, a potent antioxidant that helps fight inflammation and supports overall immune
function.
● The Spices (Nutmeg, Mace, & Cinnamon): More than just flavor enhancers, these
spices have antimicrobial properties. Nutmeg is often used as a mild sedative to promote
restful sleep, which is crucial for healing.
● The "Bushes" (Lemongrass & Mint): These cooling herbs are often used to break fevers
and settle stomachs. Their refreshing oils can also help clear the head during a cold.
● The Flowers (Sorrel/Hibiscus): The deep red calyxes of the sorrel plant are packed with
Vitamin C and antioxidants, making them a delicious and powerful immune booster.

The "Cold Buster" Brew (Respiratory Support)
This warming blend is designed to clear airways and provide a boost of Vitamin C when you're
feeling under the weather.
● Ingredients: 1-2 West Indian bay leaves (fresh or dried), 1 cinnamon stick, juice of half a
fresh lime, raw honey to taste.
● Instructions: Simmer the bay leaves and cinnamon stick in 2 cups of water for 10
minutes. Remove from heat, strain, and stir in the fresh lime juice and honey. Drink warm.

Module 3: The Science of the Steep
To extract the maximum medicinal benefit from your ingredients, you must understand the
difference between a decoction and an infusion.
● Decoction (Left Image): This method is used for hardy materials like roots (ginger,
turmeric), bark (cinnamon), and seeds. These parts of the plant need to be simmered in
boiling water for a longer period (10-20 minutes) to break down their tough cellular
structure and release their active compounds.
● Infusion (Right Image): This method is for delicate parts of the plant like leaves (mint,
bay leaf) and flowers (sorrel). Boiling destroys their volatile oils and delicate flavors.
Instead, you pour hot water over them and let them steep (or "draw") for 5-10 minutes in
a covered vessel.
Knowing which method to use is the key to a potent and effective brew.
