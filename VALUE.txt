CORE TRADITION OVERVIEW

Caribbean herbal medicine is rooted in generations of indigenous, African, and island knowledge using leaves, roots, bark, flowers, spices, and kitchen staples for healing. Remedies are commonly prepared as bush teas, decoctions, infusions, steams, tonics, and food-based remedies. These practices focus on immune strength, fever management, respiratory health, digestion, circulation, and recovery.

PREPARATION METHODS (FOR APP REFERENCE)

Infusion
Used for leaves and flowers.
Method: Pour hot (not boiling) water over herbs. Cover and steep 5–10 minutes.

Decoction
Used for roots, bark, seeds, spices.
Method: Simmer ingredients in boiling water for 10–20 minutes.

Steam Therapy
Used for congestion.
Method: Pour boiling water over herbs, inhale steam carefully.

Tonic
Strong daily or short-term brew for prevention or recovery.
